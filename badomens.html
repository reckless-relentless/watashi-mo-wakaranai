BAD OMENS

Bad Omens is an American metal band formed in 2015 in Richmond, Virginia. Fronted by vocalist Noah Sebastian, the band quickly gained attention for their cinematic sound, blending metalcore with atmospheric production, electronic elements, and emotionally driven lyrics. Over time, they’ve developed a distinct identity that moves between heavy and melodic, often within the same song.

They introduced themselves with their self-titled debut album Bad Omens, which leaned heavily into metalcore with dark themes, breakdowns, and a moody, polished sound. Early comparisons were often made to bands like Bring Me the Horizon, but it didn’t take long for them to carve out their own style. Their follow-up album Finding God Before God Finds Me showed clear growth, incorporating more melody, cleaner vocals, and experimental production choices that expanded their sound beyond traditional metalcore.

A major shift came with The Death of Peace of Mind, where the band fully embraced a more experimental and genre-blending approach. The album mixes alternative rock, electronic, and even pop influences with darker, atmospheric tones, creating a more immersive and modern sound. This release significantly increased their popularity and brought them into a wider mainstream spotlight.

What makes Bad Omens stand out is their focus on mood and emotion as much as heaviness. Their music often explores themes like isolation, mental health, relationships, and self-destruction, delivered through a mix of haunting melodies, heavy breakdowns, and layered production.
